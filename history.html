The History of Puchong

Did you know? Originally, Puchong is named after a bird.

Black-crowned Night Heron or "Burung Puchong Kuak" in Malay.


Puchong has been in existence for over 120 years, deriving its name from a bird species known to frequent riverbanks and mines. Around 1900, the indigenous people, Orang Asli, adopted the Puchong bird as a favored food source, and it was a Malay child who assigned the name to the area, a name that persists to this day.

Puchong Perdana

Puchong Perdana is a township located within the administrative jurisdiction of the Petaling District, in the state of Selangor. Puchong Perdana is one of the earliest residential areas or settlements found in Puchong due to its flat terrain. Construction began in the late 1980s, and it was initially a mining area. Puchong Perdana was also a forested area before it became a mining site.

Over time, Puchong Perdana has experienced rapid development, with balanced growth similar to other developed areas in the Klang Valley. Before entering the Puchong Perdana area, there is a beautifully situated mosque by the lake, Masjid Puchong Perdana, and a drainage system connected to the Klang River.
